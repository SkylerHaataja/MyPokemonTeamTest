TRAINER'S NOTEBOOK

From Catch to Champion

1. IV Training – Hyper Train all stats

Buy 6 Gold Bottle Caps at Delibird Presents (20k each). Fly to Montenevera, talk to the BP man with Abomasnow → Hyper Train each Pokémon. Full guide

2. EV Spreads (exact for each team member)
  • Gothitelle: 252 HP / 252 SpA / 4 SpD (Modest)
  • Mimikyu: 252 Atk / 4 SpD / 252 Spe (Jolly)
  • Iron Hands: 252 HP / 252 Atk / 4 SpD (Adamant)
  • Gholdengo: 252 HP / 252 SpA / 4 Spe (Modest)
  • Meowscarada: 252 Atk / 4 SpD / 252 Spe (Jolly)
  • Ceruledge: 252 Atk / 4 SpD / 252 Spe (Adamant)

Train using Power items or Vitamins (Calcium, Carbos etc). EV guide

3. Natures & Mints

Use Mints from Chansey Supply to lock: Modest (Gothitelle/Gholdengo), Jolly (Mimikyu/Meowscarada), Adamant (Iron Hands/Ceruledge). Mint list

4. Key Moves – where to find them

Belly Drum (Iron Hands): egg move – Mirror Herb + Hariyama.
Shadow Sneak (Mimikyu/Ceruledge): level up / TM.
Recover (Gholdengo): TM (obtain from Porto Marinada).
Flower Trick (Meowscarada): learns at Lv. 40. Full move guide

5. Singles strategy & lead choices

Lead options: Mimikyu (Disguise + Swords Dance) or Meowscarada (fast U-turn).
Mid‑game: Gholdengo blocks status; Iron Hands sets up Belly Drum vs. slower teams.
Late clean: Ceruledge (Bitter Blade recovery) or Shadow Sneak priority.
Gothitelle traps passive walls, forces favorable matchups.
Singles mechanics

6. Tera Raid adaptations

Iron Hands (Belly Drum + Drain Punch) solos most 5★ raids. Gholdengo (Nasty Plot + Metal Sound) works vs. status spammers. Raid guide
